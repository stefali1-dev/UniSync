Data Structures

Course information

The Data Structures course explores fundamental concepts related to data organization and manipulation. It covers various data structures commonly used in solving computational problems. Topics include arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, and trees. Students will learn how to implement these structures in different programming languages and understand their practical applications.

The course evaluation consists of two components:

  • Ongoing Laboratory Evaluation: Throughout the semester, there will be three laboratory exams assessing practical skills and understanding of course material.
  • Final Exam: At the end of the semester, a comprehensive final exam will take place. To pass, students need a minimum score of 4.5 in the final exam and a median score of at least 4.5 across the three laboratory exams.

In the laboratory sessions, students will delve deeper into the algorithms presented during the course. Practical exercises, problem-solving, and collaborative learning will enhance understanding of data structures.
